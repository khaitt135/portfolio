Category overview · Channel shift & market concentration

The question is whether the dominant player can grow online at the same pace as the rest of the category.

The Pattern
Why online growth is not evenly distributed
Observed Pattern

Every brand growing faster than the category leader has a higher online proportion and a sustainability angle. The pattern is not random.

The question is: can the data prove a causal link — or is this just correlation?

🌐 Conventional Leaders
High market share, moderate online proportion, steady but slower growth
The established market leaders balance in-store dominance with growing online presence. Online channel is growing for all of them, but the category leader’s online mix is below where the category is trending. Scale creates a math problem — absolute growth is substantial but % growth looks modest.
📈 Direct Competitor
Smaller share, growing faster online, higher online proportion
The category leader’s nearest challenger is growing faster overall and has a meaningfully higher online proportion. The gap in online mix is the structural advantage that’s compounding each year — and the data shows it in the Value vs YA comparison.
🌿 Eco-Forward Brands
Tiny share, explosive omni growth, sustainability-first positioning
New eco challengers are achieving triple-digit online growth from a small base. Some lead online, others lead in-store — but purchase frequency is strong across channels for both. Shoppers engage repeatedly once acquired. The occasion-per-buyer growth rate is the signal that this isn’t just trial — it’s a category shift.

⚠ The Counter-Intuitive Finding
Eco brands have a lower repeat rate than category average — and it doesn’t matter
The intuition is that sustainability buyers are loyal. The data says otherwise: eco brands show the lowest repeat rate across brand groups. Yet they have the highest omni growth. Why? Because new buyer acquisition is outpacing churn — these brands are expanding the buyer pool, not deepening loyalty.

Recommendations
Team 4 strategy · data-anchored · four pillars

Every recommendation is anchored to a data finding. These are not generic strategy bullets — each one traces back to a specific OmniShopper behavior, regression output, or category dynamic identified in the analysis.

Rec 1
📈
Expand Online Presence — Influencer & Brand Deals
Online is growing 8× faster than in-store. The category leader’s online proportion is below the category trend. Expanding online reach via influencer brand deals targets the Gen Z segment (77% want brands easily available online) and directly addresses the channel mix gap that the regression identified as the primary structural disadvantage.

Reflection & Learnings
📊
Regression is only as good as the question
The p-value of 0.00057 is meaningful because the hypothesis was precise: “does sustainability interact with online proportion to explain differential growth?” Without that framing, the same model would have been exploratory noise. Statistical rigor starts with knowing what you’re testing for — not fishing through variables.
▴
The counter-intuitive finding reframed the entire pitch
Eco brands having a lower repeat rate than the category average was the finding that changed everything. It meant the sustainability play isn’t about loyalty — it’s about acquisition. Without OmniShopper data, that reframe was invisible. The most valuable insight came from not assuming the obvious.
🔗
Two analytical lenses complement each other
Team 3’s regression answered the “whether” — is there a causal link? Team 4’s shopper behavior analysis answered the “how” — what does a brand actually need to do differently? Neither lens alone was sufficient. The integrated output is stronger than either analysis individually.
📢
SCQA turns analysis into a pitch
Situation (healthy category, digital shift) → Complication (leader growing slower than eco challengers) → Question (can data prove sustainability drives this?) → Answer (yes, p = 0.00057, here’s the four-part strategy). Structuring as a pitch — not a report — changed how the findings landed in the boardroom presentation.
