Key Learnings
What this built beyond the resume
🔎
Granularity is the edge
Campaign-level averages hide what's actually happening. SKU-level visibility reveals which products are driving spend vs generating it. Granularity isn't a reporting preference — it's what makes optimization actionable. Building reporting at that depth was the prerequisite for every decision that followed.
⏱
eCommerce runs in real time
On a Mega Day, a top-performing SKU can become a budget drain within hours. Static campaign budgets set at midnight don't adapt to that. The playbook was built because the math required it — not as a best practice exercise.
🔗
The liaison role is the highest leverage
Being the translation layer between inventory signals, brand business decisions, and media execution is where the real complexity lives. Most co-ops own one of those three. Owning all three simultaneously forces systems thinking at a different level.
🏗
Infrastructure outlasts the campaign
The ETL, SKU taxonomy, and media brief templates became the team's operational standard beyond this role. Building tools that others can use is harder than running campaigns — and more durable as a contribution.
🔍
Demand intelligence is upstream of media
Tracking competitor search volumes on Shopee to inform bidding strategy wasn't a media tactic — it was consumer intelligence work. The best media decisions are made with demand context, not just platform signals.
🌞
Scale reveals what small campaigns hide
Managing a portfolio of this size — multiple brands, categories, and platforms simultaneously — makes patterns visible that single-brand campaigns can't show: budget pacing errors, platform-specific auction dynamics, cross-category cannibalization, and how Mega Day inventory signals cascade across the whole portfolio in real time.
